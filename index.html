How It Works

Your room is lying to you

Every room adds its own acoustic signature: bass modes, reflections, comb filtering. Two studios with the same monitors can sound completely different. You’re mixing blind.

Perfect Soup measures how your speakers actually behave in your specific room, from multiple listening positions. Then it computes filters that correct the combined speaker + room response back to flat. The result: what you hear is what’s in the mix.

Everything you need for accurate monitoring

From stereo desktop setups to 11.1.6 Atmos and beyond, Perfect Soup handles it all.

Room + Speaker Correction

Advanced algorithms identify and correct room modes and early reflections, in addition to response linearization, for accurate reproduction in any listening environment.

Automatic Crossover Parameterization

Manages the handoff between main speakers and subwoofers, aligning phase, timing, and level for seamless transitions.

Distortion-Aware Optimization

Analyzes each speaker’s distortion profile and plays to its strengths, shaping corrections that minimize distortion rather than blindly flattening response.

Zero-Assumption Design

Zero-prior phylosophy doesn’t make any assumptions about your room or speakers. Every correction is data-driven from end to end.

Multi-Seat Perfection

Optimizes for every seat in the room: engineer, producer, client. Nobody gets left out, and every position sounds accurate.

Seamless Integration

Apply calibration as a VST/AU plugin in your DAW with auto render bypass. No workflow changes required, no extra hardware needed.

Four steps to calibrated sound

The whole process takes about an hour

1

Measure

The Calibration Wizard plays test sweeps through each speaker while your measurement mic captures the room response at multiple positions.

2

Upload

Measurements are packaged and uploaded to the Umami Audio cloud. Name them, manage them from your dashboard.

3

Process

Our GPU-accelerated MIMO engine computes optimal filters, correcting room modes, crossovers, timing, and response per speaker group.

4

Apply

Load the calibration file in the Calibration Loader plugin on your DAW monitor bus. Calibrated monitoring, immediately.
